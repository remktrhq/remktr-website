Stay Ahead: SP-API Deprecation Deadlines Loom

Written by Jacob Heinz | Jun 25, 2025 8:19:19 PM

Imagine you're cruising down the info highway, and your trusty roadmap – the SP-API resources – gets an update. Yep, those old resources you've been counting on are wrapping up their journey. Important to note, they'll keep working… until they don't.

The magic stops on their removal date! Want the scoop on these deprecation dates? The Deprecations table is your new go-to. It's like your crystal ball for SP-API’s future tweaks, giving you a heads-up before anything drops off the radar.

The clock's ticking towards June 2025. What's the buzz? Well, JackE_Amazon shares a big memo from June 25th, 2025. Picture this – March 18, 2024, marked the goodbye for several feed type values. But here's the twist: their exit got pushed back – not to March 31, 2025, but now June 30, 2025! Mark it down, folks!

  • POSTPRODUCTDATA
  • POSTINVENTORYAVAILABILITY_DATA
  • POSTPRODUCTOVERRIDES_DATA
  • POSTPRODUCTPRICING_DATA
  • POSTPRODUCTIMAGE_DATA
  • POSTPRODUCTRELATIONSHIP_DATA
  • POSTFLATFILEINVLOADERDATA
  • POSTFLATFILEBOOKLOADERDATA
  • POSTFLATFILECONVERGENCELISTINGS_DATA
  • POSTFLATFILELISTINGSDATA
  • POSTFLATFILEPRICEANDQUANTITYONLYUPDATE_DATA
  • POSTUIEEBOOKLOADER_DATA

What does this mean for you? To dodge service interruptions, time to shift gears. Start migrating any integrations relying on these operations to the newer, shinier JSON-based alternatives. Trust us, staying ahead never felt this good.

Next up, we have the Catalog Items API v0 taking a bow, set to leave on March 31, 2026. Let's be real; nobody likes surprise news, so keep an eye on timelines to save you a bunch of headaches.

In the Reports department, some report type values are taking their final curtain call, set for March 25, 2026:

  • GETV2SETTLEMENTREPORTDATAFLATFILE
  • GETV2SETTLEMENTREPORTDATA_XML

Heads up, those comfy report types you love won’t be around forever!

Oh, and if you're tracking Product Pricing, here's a twist – by September 30, 2025, products won't reference prices named CompetitivePriceThreshold. Yep, change is coming fast, and it could roll past your edge if you don't adapt.
