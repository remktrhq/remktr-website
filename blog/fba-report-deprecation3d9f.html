Understanding Amazon FBA Report Deprecations: What You Need to Know

Written by Jacob Heinz | Jun 25, 2025 6:21:10 PM

The Amazon FBA world feels like a crazy web of logistics, sales, and managing stuff. The reports are like the veins that keep sellers up-to-date and nimble. Imagine having your own spy system that watches every tiny thing happening in your business.

But, big changes are on the horizon. Amazon is cutting out some major reports, namely the Subscribe and Save (SnS) Forecast and Performance Reports by 2025.

You might think, "Why should I care?" Well, these reports give you secret intel on subscription habits and performance numbers. It's like trying to bake without a recipe — losing these reports is like flying blind.

But wait! Don't freak out. You can do plenty to get ready for this shift. In this post, we're diving into what these changes mean, why they're happening, and how you can roll with it. Think of it as a chance to remake your data plan, so you still bake the perfect cake — golden and fluffy, every time.

TL;DR

  • Amazon is ditching key FBA reports related to Subscribe and Save by December 2025.
  • Sellers need to rethink plans to handle the loss of key numbers.
  • Understanding this shakeup is key to keep your sales edge.
  • We’ll explore other data tools in detail.

Why the Reports Are Vanishing

Amazon FBA's reporting tools are getting a shake-up, and chopping the Subscribe and Save reports is part of this new plan. Info on renewals and performance is must-have stuff, but Amazon wants things to be smooth, maybe more so than having duplicate info.

Experts like Sarah Petersen, a top e-commerce consultant, say, "Amazon is probably trimming to shine a light on fancier, tightly integrated reports. Sellers will lean on larger, more well-rounded data instead of niche stuff."

Sellers love these reports to track subscription sales and know customer loyalty cycles, key for the best stock and marketing moves. Their removal could hint at Amazon leaning towards bigger data stories. It’s like scrapping an old jukebox to make space for a top-notch sound system.

Where to Find Your Data

After December 2025, FBA sellers need to slowly let go of relying on specific reports like the SnS Performance Report. Never fear, though. There's a whole other world of data to dig into.

  1. Fulfillment Reports: These offer details on orders, shipments, and items — super useful for getting the logistics right. Imagine knowing exactly when your package leaves the warehouse and arrives at your buyer's door.
  2. Inventory Reports: Perfect for keeping tabs on stock levels and movement that impact subscription-based sales. It’s like checking the pantry before grocery shopping so you don’t come back with yet another bottle of ketchup.
  3. Sales Reports: They sum up customer orders, letting you adjust marketing fast. With these, you can spot trends quicker than summer fashion going old.

By using these reports, sellers can build a strong data backbone that's not just reacting to changes from Amazon but also taking charge strategically.

Alternative Solutions and Tools

Amazon might be nudging you away from certain data, but other options are out there. Tons of tools now can grab Amazon data and sync easily with other business tools, which may be a game-changer.

  • Helium 10 and Jungle Scout are sought-after tools that offer a full suite of analysis on sales trends and performance. Think of them as your business fortune teller.
  • Data analytics platforms like Google Data Studio allow sellers to see data from different places, adjust reports to fit their business style, and dig out key insights. You can turn raw data into an engaging, interactive tale.

These platforms are key for sellers aiming to stay ahead, using data to make smart moves and grow–especially as Amazon reshapes its data report offer.

  • Use other Amazon reports to cover data shortfalls.
  • Lean on third-party tools for merged reports and insights.
